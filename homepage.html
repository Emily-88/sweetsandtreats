Recipe of the Day

Today we are making something you can never go wrong with, cookies! We'll be starting with a base recipe, and I'll be suggesting some mix-ins, but feel free to change them up, or add none at all!

Base Recipe

  • 1 1/2 cups unsalted butter (melted and browned in a saucepan, then cooled to room temperature)
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 cup cake or pastry flour Here's how you make cake flour from AP flour and cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon salt (flakey kosher if available)

Instructions

  • Melt butter in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Simmer until butter starts to turn a warm amber color and gives off a nutty aroma. Watch it carefully, it changes from a warm amber color to brown very quickly. Allow to cool to room temperature.
  • Once it is cooled, add brown sugar and sugar, then blend for 4 minutes. Scrape down the bowl. Add eggs one at a time, mixing after each. Add vanilla extract and mix.
  • Stir in cake or pastry flour, all-purpose flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Mix until just combined.

Mix-Ins (Optional)

  • 1 1/2 cups chocolate chunks
  • Sweetened coconut flakes, toasted
  • 1 cup dark chocolate chips

Baking

  • If able, wrap and chill your dough for 24-48 hours, then remove and allow to soften at room temperature prior to scooping onto a cookie sheet and baking. Oftentimes I make these last minute so I just pop them in the fridge for 30 minutes prior to baking. Here's why I think this is essential.
  • Heat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Place parchment paper on cookie sheets. Scoop cookies to desired size, I usually make mine small, and it makes around 48 cookies.
  • If mixing in the dark chocolate chips, feel free to sprinkle some flakey sea salt on the top before baking.
  • Bake for 8-10 minutes or 11-13 minutes if you make them larger.
  • Once out, allow to cool completely on the cookie sheets.
  • Enjoy!
